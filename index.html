The city

The largest state in Brazil is São Paulo.

Population

The population of the state of São Paulo is approximately 47 million people. Meanwhile, the city of São Paulo has about 13 million inhabitants

Modern architectures

Hotel Unique

Designed by Ruy Ohtake, the hotel has a shape that resembles a ship or a slice of watermelon, depending on the perspective, and is a notable example of contemporary and innovative design.

São Paulo Museum of Art (MASP)

Designed by Lina Bo Bardi, MASP is known for its concrete and glass structure supported by four red pillars, with a free span of 74 metres.

Tomie Ohtake Institute

This cultural centre was designed by architect Ruy Ohtake and is dedicated to presenting art, architecture, and design exhibitions. Featuring a facade marked by colourful, undulating shapes, the institute is considered one of the most expressive examples of contemporary architecture in the city and stands out in the urban landscape of São Paulo.

historical buildings

São Paulo Municipal Theatre

Opened in 1911, this theatre is one of the most important landmarks of the city and a prime example of eclectic architecture, with influences from the Paris Opera. It hosted the Modern Art Week in 1922, a crucial event for the cultural modernisation of Brazil.

São Paulo Cathedral

Located in the heart of São Paulo, the Metropolitan Cathedral, or São Paulo Cathedral, is one of the five largest neo-Gothic temples in the world. Construction began in 1913 and was completed in 1954, in time for the city's 400th anniversary.

Lady of Brazil Parish

located in Jardim América in São Paulo, was built in 1940. This Catholic temple stands out for its blend of neocolonial and neobaroque styles, with two symmetrical towers and an interior richly decorated with stained glass and paintings. It is a popular venue for weddings due to its architectural beauty and the sacred atmosphere it offers.

Beaches, Parks and Forests

Ibirapuera Park

Considered the best park in São Paulo, Ibirapuera is a central green space that offers cultural activities, museums, leisure areas, and large spaces for walking and sports. It is one of the city's symbols.

Cantareira Forest Reserve

One of the largest urban forests in the world, located in the metropolitan region of São Paulo. The Cantareira Forest Reserve is a natural refuge that offers hiking trails, observation points, and rich biodiversity.

Bonete Beach

Located in Ilhabela, this beach is accessible only by trails or by boat, which keeps it preserved and tranquil. It is known for its untouched natural beauty, strong surf waves, and a small fishing community.

Curiosities of São Paulo

Summer

It is the hottest season and also quite humid. Temperatures are high, often exceeding 30°C. Heavy rains in the late afternoon are common.

Winter

It is the coldest season. In São Paulo, the cold is not very intense, but temperatures can drop to about 10°C on some days, especially at night and early morning. Winter is drier, with little rain.

Autumn

During this time, the weather begins to become milder. Temperatures drop slightly compared to summer, and the rainfall starts to decrease.

Spring

The weather gradually warms up, gearing up for summer. Rainfall begins to increase again, especially in November.

Virado à Paulista

This dish is traditionally served on Mondays. It consists of rice, bean purée, sautéed collard greens, pork chop, fried egg, and breaded banana.

Feijoada

Although it is a popular dish throughout Brazil, feijoada holds a special place in São Paulo cuisine. It is a mixture of black beans with various cuts of pork and beef, served with rice, toasted cassava flour (farofa), collard greens, and orange.

Pastel de Feira

Found at street markets, the pastel is a thin fried dough filled with a variety of ingredients such as minced meat, cheese, heart of palm, and cod.

Campos do Jordão

This mountainous city is known as the 'Brazilian Switzerland' due to its cold climate and European architecture. It is a popular destination during the winter, offering beautiful mountain landscapes, hiking trails, waterfalls, and a variety of charming restaurants and shops.

Ilhabela

Located on the northern coast of São Paulo, Ilhabela is a paradisiacal island with over 40 beaches, lush waterfalls, and trails through the Atlantic Forest. It is a paradise for nature lovers and water sports enthusiasts, such as diving, snorkelling, and sailing.

Serra da Mantiqueira

This mountain range spans parts of São Paulo, Minas Gerais, and Rio de Janeiro, offering breathtaking landscapes such as mountains, valleys, forests, and waterfalls. Cities like São Bento do Sapucaí and Monte Verde are popular destinations in the region, ideal for those seeking tranquility and contact with nature.

Japanese

São Paulo is home to the largest Japanese community outside Japan. The Japanese influence in the city is evident in neighbourhoods like Liberdade, where Japanese shops, restaurants, markets, and cultural events can be found. Additionally, São Paulo has a large number of Japanese descendants who keep their cultural heritage alive through festivals, celebrations, and traditions.

Italian

The Italian influence in São Paulo dates back to the late 19th century when Italian immigrants began arriving in the city. Italian culture is celebrated in São Paulo through gastronomy, with the presence of numerous authentic Italian restaurants, as well as in festivals, such as the traditional Feast of Our Lady of Achiropita, held in the Bixiga neighbourhood.

Lebanese

São Paulo is home to a large community of Lebanese immigrants, who brought with them their rich culture. Lebanese influence can be seen in various aspects of life in the city, including gastronomy, with the presence of many restaurants serving dishes such as esfiha, kibbeh, and hummus.

Avenida Paulista

Avenida Paulista is one of the main financial and cultural centres of São Paulo city. It is known for its modern architecture, shopping centres, restaurants, and vibrant nightlife. The highlight of the avenue is the São Paulo Museum of Art (MASP), which houses one of the most important collections of Western art in Latin America, including works by renowned artists such as Van Gogh, Renoir, and Picasso.

Brotas

Brotas is known as the adventure capital of São Paulo. Located in the centre of the state, the city offers a variety of outdoor activities such as rafting, zip-lining, canyoning, and hiking. In addition to the extreme sports, Brotas also has beautiful waterfalls and natural landscapes, making it a popular destination for those seeking adrenaline and contact with nature.

Serra do Mar e Ferrovia Paranapiacaba

The Serra do Mar is a mountain range covered by the Atlantic Forest, offering beautiful landscapes, trails, and waterfalls. The Paranapiacaba Railway is an old train line that cuts through the Serra do Mar and is known for its tunnels, bridges, and breathtaking scenery. Paranapiacaba, a historic village at the top of the mountain, preserves the architecture and charm of the colonial period and is an interesting destination for history and nature lovers
